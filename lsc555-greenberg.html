Remarks on Joan Didion

Joan Didion is one of my favorite English prose stylists. It was recently reported that some of her diary entries are going to be published. While Didion herself would probably not have been happy that these diary entries are going to be published--given what she wrote about the publication of a writer's unpublished works, I am nevertheless looking forward to reading them. (I guess that makes me the kind of person, according to Hemingway, as cited in Didion's piece, "who would cheat a man at cards or rifle his desk or wastebasket or read his personal letters.")

If you have never read Joan Didion, I recommend that you start with the following works of nonfiction, despite the fact that she saw herself primarily as a fiction writer who wrote nonfiction in her 'down' time.

  • Slouching Towards Bethlehem
  • The White Album
  • Where I Was From

If you decide to try Didion, I'd be very interested to learn what you think of her writing!
